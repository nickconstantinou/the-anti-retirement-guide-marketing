FAQ

Questions couples ask before retirement

We can just talk about this when the time comes, right?
Most couples think they will — and then discover they’re living in two completely different futures. The time to have this conversation is while you’re both still working and have the bandwidth for an honest, low-stakes discussion. Not when one of you is already retired and feeling the pressure.
What if we disagree about what retirement looks like?
Disagreement isn’t the problem — silence is. The guide doesn’t promise you’ll always want the same things. It gives you a framework for navigating the differences before they become sources of resentment rather than conversations.
Is this just for people approaching retirement?
The earlier the better. Couples in their 40s and 50s who work through these questions have significantly more time to align their expectations — and significantly less to unlearn when the moment arrives.
