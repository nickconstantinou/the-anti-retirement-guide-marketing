Frequently Asked Questions

Straight answers to the questions people are actually afraid to ask.

  • Isn’t anti-retirement just a fancy word for not retiring?

    Not quite. “Anti-retirement” isn’t about refusing to stop working — it’s about refusing to stop mattering. You can step back from a career, wind down a business, or exit a partnership and still be deeply engaged. The difference is whether that engagement is self-directed or just a comfortable default. This guide is about the former.

  • I’m already exhausted. I don’t want to take on more projects.

    Good. Neither does this guide. Anti-retirement isn’t about adding more to your plate — it’s about removing the things that drain you and creating the space for what actually lights you up. The Discovery Deck helps you figure out the difference before you commit to anything.

  • I’m not a business owner — does this apply to me?

    Absolutely. While Tony and Kevin are ex-owners, Christine and Martin are a GP and a corporate director — and their stories resonate with anyone who’s built their identity around professional achievement and is now confronting what’s left when that’s no longer the main thing. The design thinking framework works regardless of your background.

  • What if my family thinks I should just enjoy retirement?

    Loving families often conflate “rest” with “care” because they’ve absorbed the same cultural script. This guide doesn’t ask you to ignore their concerns — it gives you a language to explain what you’re actually searching for, and a framework that helps them understand why restlessness isn’t a disease to be cured. It’s a design clue.
